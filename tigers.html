TIGERS

Tigers (Panthera tigris) are the largest of all wild cats and are native to parts of Asia, including India, Russia, and Southeast Asia. Known for their striking orange coats with black stripes, each tiger has a unique pattern like a fingerprint. They are solitary, powerful predators that hunt deer, wild boar, and other large animals. Tigers are also strong swimmers and often cool off in water, unlike most other cats. They can live between 10 to 15 years in the wild. Sadly, all tiger subspecies are currently endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.
