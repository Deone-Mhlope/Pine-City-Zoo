KOALA

The koala (Phascolarctos cinereus) is a tree-dwelling marsupial native to eastern Australia. Often mistakenly called a "koala bear," the koala is not a bear but a marsupial, meaning it carries its young in a pouch. Koalas are known for their thick, fluffy fur, large round ears, and gentle appearance. They live in eucalyptus forests and feed almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves, which are tough and low in nutrients. Because of their low-energy diet, koalas sleep up to 18–22 hours a day to conserve energy. Koalas are solitary animals and have a strong sense of smell to help them select the best leaves. In the wild, they can live up to 10–15 years, but their populations are threatened by habitat loss, disease, and bushfires.
