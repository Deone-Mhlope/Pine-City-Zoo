GORILLA

Gorillas are large, powerful primates native to the tropical and subtropical forests of central Africa. There are two main species: the western gorilla (Gorilla gorilla) and the eastern gorilla (Gorilla beringei). Gorillas are primarily herbivores, feeding on fruits, leaves, and stems. They are highly intelligent, capable of using tools and communicating through complex vocalizations and gestures. Sharing about 98% of their DNA with humans, gorillas are one of our closest relatives in the animal kingdom. In the wild, they live around 35 to 40 years, and even longer in captivity.
