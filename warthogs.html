WARTHOGS

Warthogs (Phacochoerus africanus) are wild members of the pig family found in the savannas and grasslands of sub-Saharan Africa. Recognized by their large tusks and warty faces, warthogs may look fierce, but they are generally non-aggressive. They are omnivores, feeding on grass, roots, berries, and small animals. Warthogs often use their strong snouts to dig for food and take shelter in abandoned burrows, especially those made by aardvarks. When grazing, they frequently kneel on their front legs. Warthogs live for about 12 to 15 years in the wild.
